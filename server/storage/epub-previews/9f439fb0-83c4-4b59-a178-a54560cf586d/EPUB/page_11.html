In a figurative way George Moore was right when he declared that ‘Miss Austen was the inventor of the formula whereby domestic life may be described; every one of us without exception, Balzac and Turgenev as well as Mrs. Henry Wood and Anthony Trollope, is indebted to her.’ Though Balzac may never have heard of Jane Austen’s work, this remark is essentially true. Pride and Prejudice was the first of Jane Austen’s novels to be written, and the original draft dates from her twenty-first year. On her own imagination the characters seem to have had the strongest hold of all her creations, and with her family the book was a favourite. So much so that Jane Austen used to joke with agreeable gravity about the personalities of Elizabeth and Jane Bennet in correspondence with her sister. In a letter from London in May 1813 she pretended to have been searching for likenesses of the Bennet sisters in the art exhibitions of the time. ‘Henry and I went to the exhibition in Spring Gardens,’ she wrote, ‘I was well pleased with a small portrait of Mrs. Bingley, excessively like her. She is dressed in a white gown with green ornaments, which convince me of what | always supposed, that green was a favourite colour with her. I dare say Mrs. Darcy will be in yellow.’ But in a postscript there was a disappointment to record. At the exhibition of Sir Joshua Reynolds’s paintings, ‘there was nothing like Mrs. Darcy. I can only imagine that Mr. Darcy prizes any picture of her too much to like it should be exposed to the public eye. I can imagine he would have that sort of feeling—that mixture of love, pride and delicacy.’ When characters so dwell in their creator’s mind—and that during one of her rare shopping expeditions to London—it is no wonder that they live on for posterity. In the year of this letter Pride and Prejudice was published, sixteen years after it was completed. The original draft, called First Impressions, had been offered to a publisher by Jane Austen’s father in 1797, and turned down. From Jane Austen’s letters to her family it is clear that this draft was considerably revised.
