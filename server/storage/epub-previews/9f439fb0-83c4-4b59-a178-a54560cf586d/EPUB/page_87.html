With such rivals for the notice of the fairas Mr. Wickham and the officers, Mr. Collins seemed to sink into insignificance; to the young ladies he certainly was nothing; but he had still at intervals a kind listener in Mrs. Philips, and was, by her watchfulness, most abundantly supplied with coffee and muffin. When the card-tables were placed, he had an opportunity of obliging her in return, by sitting down to whist. “I know little of the game at present,’ said he, “but I shall be glad to improve myself, for in my situation in life——’ Mrs. Philips was very thankful for his compliance, but could not wait for his reason. Mr. Wickham did not play at whist, and with ready delight was he received at the other table between Elizabeth and Lydia.
