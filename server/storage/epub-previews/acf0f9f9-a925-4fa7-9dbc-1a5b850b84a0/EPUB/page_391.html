My aunt’s intelligence had given me hope, and I was determined at once to know everything.’ “Lady Catherine has been of infinite use, which ought to make her happy, for she loves to be of use. But tell me, what did you come down to Netherfield for? Was it merely to ride to Longbourn, and be embarrassed? or had you intended any more serious consequences?’ “My real purpose was to see you, and to judge, if I could, whether I might ever hope to make you love me. My avowed one, or what I avowed to myself, was to see whether your sister was still partial to Bingley, and, if she were, to make the confession to him which I have since made.’ “Shall you ever have courage to announce to Lady Catherine what is to befall her?’ ‘Tam more likely to want time than courage, Elizabeth. But it ought to be done; and if you will give me a sheet of paper, it shall be done directly.’ ‘And if I had not a letter to write myself, I might sit by you, and admire the evenness of your writing, as another young lady once did.
